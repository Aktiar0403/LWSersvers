Analysis Logic — How This Comparison Works

This comparison engine evaluates alliances or warzones using time-adjusted player power and population structure, rather than static or self-reported totals. Each player’s strength is normalized into an Effective Power value to compensate for stale confirmations while preserving end-game saturation effects.

Effective Power is computed using tier-dependent weekly growth rates, applied only when power data is unconfirmed. Lower-power players grow faster, while high-end players follow diminishing growth curves to prevent artificial whale inflation.

Players are stratified into discrete power tiers (Mega Whales, Whales, Sharks, Piranhas, Shrimps) to expose structural composition rather than raw totals. Comparative charts visualize tier distribution to highlight elite concentration and depth balance between the two selections.

The Top-10 list represents the effective frontline core, extracted by sorting players on Effective Power. First-squad capability is estimated using range-based heuristics derived from observed end-game builds, avoiding false precision while preserving tactical relevance.

Final comparison outputs include total effective power, elite density, frontline dominance, and relative structural advantage. Results are analytical in nature and describe comparative strength, not deterministic combat outcomes.

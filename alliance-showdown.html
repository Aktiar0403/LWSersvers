ACIS Methodology — Combat Inference Framework

v1.3.7

The Alliance Combat Intelligence System (ACIS) is a multi-layered analytical framework derived from aggregated Top-200 Warzone datasets, engineered to estimate alliance-level combat readiness under sustained, loss-inclusive engagement models.

Ⅰ. Effective Combat Power (ECP)

Individual player power vectors are normalized via temporal decay correction, confirmation weighting, and tier-dependent growth caps to suppress stale telemetry and asymmetrical inflation effects.

Ⅱ. Frontline Engagement Vector (FEV)

Primary strike capability is modeled through averaged First-Squad Power distributions, approximating early-phase battlefield dominance and momentum acquisition probability.

Ⅲ. Depth Elasticity Index (DEI)

Bench-to-active ratios are evaluated to quantify reinforcement elasticity, modeling how efficiently an alliance absorbs attritional losses without structural destabilization.

Ⅳ. Stability Coefficient (σ)

Organizational resilience is expressed as a stability coefficient, incorporating power dispersion entropy, frontline concentration risk, and cascade-failure susceptibility metrics.

Ⅴ. Composite Combat Index (CCI)

Final alliance strength is synthesized through a weighted composite of ECP, FEV, DEI, and σ, producing a normalized Combat Index suitable for cross-alliance comparative inference.

Probabilistic win likelihoods are derived from pairwise CCI ratios, transformed through a logistic response function to preserve uncertainty and avoid deterministic overfitting in near-equivalent scenarios.

ACIS outputs represent probabilistic readiness estimations rather than deterministic outcomes. Tactical leadership, diplomacy, coordination, and real-time execution dynamics remain intentionally non-modeled variables.

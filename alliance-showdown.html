ACIS — Alliance Combat Intelligence System

Framework v1.3.7

The Alliance Combat Intelligence System (ACIS) is a multi-model, multi-variable combat inference framework derived from aggregated Top-200 Warzone telemetry. It is engineered to estimate alliance-level combat readiness under sustained, loss-inclusive engagement conditions, rather than superficial power comparisons.

CCI = α·ECP + β·FEV + γ·DEI + δ·σ Composite Combat Index (CCI) synthesis model

Ⅰ. Effective Combat Power (ECP)

Individual power vectors are transformed into Effective Combat Power using temporal decay correction, confirmation-weighted trust coefficients, and tier-dependent growth caps. This suppresses stale telemetry, artificial inflation, and asymmetric whale bias.

Ⅱ. Frontline Engagement Vector (FEV)

Frontline strength is modeled through averaged First-Squad Power distributions, representing early-phase engagement dominance and momentum acquisition probability during initial conflict windows.

Ⅲ. Depth Elasticity Index (DEI)

Bench-to-active force ratios are evaluated to quantify reinforcement elasticity — the alliance’s ability to absorb attritional losses without immediate structural degradation.

Ⅳ. Stability Coefficient (σ)

Organizational resilience is expressed via a stability coefficient, incorporating power dispersion entropy, frontline concentration risk, and cascading failure susceptibility under prolonged pressure.

Ⅴ. Probabilistic Outcome Modeling

Win likelihoods are derived from pairwise Composite Combat Index ratios, transformed through a logistic response function to preserve uncertainty and avoid deterministic overfitting in near-equivalent engagements.

System Architecture & Intelligence Layer

ACIS is architected and designed by Akki, with analytical reasoning executed via a version-pinned, multi-model intelligence stack. Core structured inference is distributed across GPT-4.1.x reasoning models, GPT-4o (o-series) multimodal inference units, and GPT-4-class analytical variants, each operating under constrained prompt scopes to enforce deterministic step decomposition and minimize stochastic drift. Probabilistic outcome weighting is delegated to decision-transformer ensembles, while uncertainty bounds and variance propagation are evaluated through Monte Carlo–driven statistical simulation engines. Cross-model outputs are reconciled through a dedicated inference arbitration layer, responsible for confidence normalization, hypothesis conflict resolution, and final signal fusion prior to Composite Combat Index (CCI) synthesis.

ACIS outputs represent probabilistic readiness estimations rather than deterministic predictions. Tactical leadership, diplomacy, coordination, morale, and real-time decision-making remain intentionally non-modeled variables.

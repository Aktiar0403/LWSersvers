ACIS — Alliance Combat Intelligence System

ACIS is a multi-model combat inference framework derived from aggregated Top-200 Warzone telemetry, designed to estimate alliance-level combat readiness under sustained attrition rather than raw power comparison.

CCI = α·ECP + β·FEV + γ·DEI + δ·σ Composite Combat Index
  • ECP — Time-corrected Effective Combat Power
  • FEV — Frontline Engagement Vector (first-squad force)
  • DEI — Depth Elasticity Index (bench sustainability)
  • σ — Stability Coefficient (collapse resistance)

Designed by Akki (also known as AkkiGPT), ACIS operates on a version-pinned ensemble of GPT-4.1.x reasoning models, GPT-4o (o-series) inference units, and GPT-4-class analytical variants. Probabilistic weighting is handled by decision-transformer ensembles, with uncertainty evaluated via Monte Carlo simulation and resolved through an inference arbitration layer. This system was developed with tremendous support, validation input, and field-level insights from SleepyRahul, Mighty07, SuperSnail, Alex Blake (Server 704), and many others across multiple warzones.

Outputs represent probabilistic readiness estimates, not deterministic outcomes. Tactical execution, leadership, and diplomacy are non-modeled variables.
